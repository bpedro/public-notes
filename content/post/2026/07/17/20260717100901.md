Today, I joined a few pieces that looked totally disconnected.

I was reading some random article about how AI and, in particular, the specification-driven development (SDD) methodology can open software development to non-technical people. The first step of SDD is to define the specification of the software to be developed. Who has better knowledge of what should be built than someone who has expertise in the area?

I immediately thought about the kinds of profiles that thrive in an AI-oriented world and job market. Expert generalists, or T-shaped professionals, are probably going to rule this AI wave. There's no room or time for specialists. Why? Because writing code is cheap. Translating is cheap. Doing things is cheap. You know what isn't cheap? Understanding what to build and understanding why you should build it. That's something an expert generalist can do better than a specialist.

And then I thought that SDD can be used by expert generalists to design and build APIs. APIs that really make sense and offer value to consumers. Expert generalists have the knowledge of their area of expertise and are generalist enough to understand what API operations they should build.
