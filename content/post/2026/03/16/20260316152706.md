Teams often use internal jargon or project code names for their public endpoints. This makes it impossible for an outside developer to understand what the service does without reading pages of docs. If the name is confusing, the adoption will be low.

My work in API design shows that clarity beats cleverness every time. I’d focus on using a consistent, resource-based naming convention that mirrors the user's mental model.
